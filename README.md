### Hi there 👋

This is a personal biography of Dong Zhou. I recently left Microsoft Research to found a new hedge fund company in China Shanghai. Stay tuned :hugs:!


### Education

I obtained both my bachelor's degree and master's degree from [Peking University](https://english.pku.edu.cn/), in July 2016 and July 2019 respectively. 
Throughout my bachelor degree, I learned a lot about natural science and later joined [Prof. Yanyi Huang's Lab](http://gene.pku.edu.cn) for scientific research.
It's also at that time I realized how hard it is to make a significant breakthrough in science and turned to pursue my career in engineering.
I jumped into the data science major for my graduate education and also found my favorite field: quantitative investing + machine learning (QIML).
I was also so fortunate to be supervised by [Prof. Weinan E](http://web.math.princeton.edu/~weinan/) for this research direction.


### Career

I became a Researcher at [Machine Learning Group](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/group/machine-learning-research-group/), 
Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA) in July 2019, right after my graduation from Peking University. During this period, I worked on many 
QIML research topics including [stock prediction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.12950), [covariance estimation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.05201), 
and [order execution](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.10860). Beyond research, I also designed many practical solutions for external 
industry partners ([ChinaAMC](https://www.chinaamc.com/fund/007994/index.shtml) & Taiping Asset Management) and
internal partners ([MSN Money](https://www.msn.com/en-us/money)).

Before joining MSRA as a full-time employee, I also spent more than one year as an intern at the same group. 
I created [Qlib](https://github.com/microsoft/qlib) since March 2018 and developed Qlib versions up to 0.2.2 during this internship period. 
In these early versions I prototyped the entire QIML workflow from data to model to backtest. (These commits are not preserved when we open sourced Qlib).
