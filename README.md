### Hi there 👋

This is a biography of Dong Zhou (evanzd). At the time of writing, I have decided to left my current position in Microsoft Research
to found a new hedge fund company with two partners in China Shanghai. Stay tuned :hugs:!


### Education

I obtained both my bachelor's degree and master's degree from [Peking University](https://english.pku.edu.cn/), in July 2016 and July 2019 respectively. 
Throughout my bachelor degree, I learned a lot on natural science and later joined [Prof. Yanyi Huang's Lab](http://gene.pku.edu.cn) for scientific research.
It's also that time I realized how hard it is to make a significant breakthrough in science and turned to pursue my career in engineering.
I jumped into the data science major for my graduate education and also found my favorite field, quantitative investing + machine learning (QIML).
I was also so fortunate to be supervised by [Prof. Weinan E](http://web.math.princeton.edu/~weinan/) for this research direction.


### Career

I became a Researcher at [Machine Learning Group](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/group/machine-learning-research-group/), Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA) 
in July 2019, right after my graduation from Peking University. During this period, I focused on many QIML research directions including 
[stock prediction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.12950), [covariance estimation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.05201), 
and [order execution](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.10860). Beyond research, I also designed practical solutions for external 
partners ([ChinaAMC](https://www.chinaamc.com/fund/007994/index.shtml) and Taiping Asset Management) and
internal partners ([MSN Money](https://www.msn.com/en-us/money)).

Before joining MSRA as a full-time employee, I also spend more than one year as an intern in the same team. 
I created [Qlib](https://github.com/microsoft/qlib) since March 2018. I created the storage design, 
factor expression engine, datasets (Alpha158 & Alpha360), machine learning models (LGBM & GRU), strategy (TopkDropout) and backtest. 
Unfortunately, when we open source Qlib in Sept. 2020, all history commits include mine cannot be preserved.
